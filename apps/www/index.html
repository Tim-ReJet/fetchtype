typography validation for design systems

Most of what your users do is read.

Typography is the largest functional surface of any interface. Most teams never validate any of it.

what fetchtype catches
$ fetchtype validate --input tokens.json

  ✗ contrast — 4.0:1 on #6b7280 (requires 4.5:1)
  ⚠ body line-height — 1.2 (minimum 1.5)
  ⚠ button font-size — 12px (minimum 14px)
  ⚠ prose width — 92ch (maximum 75ch)

  0 passed · 1 error · 3 warnings

These are testable failures, not taste.

the drift — Your designer specifies 1.5 line-height. Your developer ships 1.2. Nobody catches it because there's nothing to catch it.

the cause — Type scales, vertical rhythm, font loading, WCAG thresholds — expertise most teams don't have.

the cost — If it's hard, teams skip it. Safe font, arbitrary sizes, generic product.

③

Three commands. That's the workflow.

1

fetchtype init

Start from a preset or describe what you're building.

fetchtype init --preset dashboard
fetchtype init --prompt "modern SaaS with dark mode"
2

fetchtype validate

13 rules. Exit code 1 on failure. Works as a CI gate.

fetchtype validate -i fetchtype.tokens.json
3

fetchtype build

Export to whatever your stack needs.

fetchtype build -i fetchtype.tokens.json --format tailwind

Wire it into CI in four lines.

# .github/workflows/typography.yml
- uses: actions/checkout@v4
- uses: pnpm/action-setup@v2
- run: pnpm install
- run: fetchtype validate -i fetchtype.tokens.json --github
pnpm add -D fetchtype
